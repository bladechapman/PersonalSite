About

HI, I'm Blade! FUN FACT! I share my name with a cutting utensil! I've played the violin for 10 years and love locking myself in my room to code, so obviously I'm one of them cool kids.

My focus at the moment is on client-side development. I love building clever logic into front-facing software as well as creating enjoyable user experiences. My strengths include iOS development, Java, and C++ in addition to experience with C and the web-languages.

Currently I'm a sophomore in Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Most of my time ends up going to tinkering, schoolwork or working on fun personal projects, but I enjoy hashing out a plan for world domination every once in a while.

Projects

Whether for work, school, or play, I love creating! Here are a few of my favorite projects from recent memory. You can check out an even better profile of my work and the people I love working with at my GitHub page!

Classkick

Software engineering intern for 1871 startup Classkick, a mobile education platform designed to give students instant and effective feedback. I worked on a small, fast-paced mobile development team to implement features and infrastructure and carry the app to launch. Check them out at getclasskick.com, or download it for iPad here!

Toss

IEEE CapitalOne 2013 Hackathon First Place! Allows user to draw and send images from a mobile device to a computer's clipboard instantly! My work included handling network protocols, encoding, and custom drawing algorithms for the iOS app. Expect to see it out on the app store soon!

Armagetra-Snake

Powered by HTML5, Armagetra-Snake uses your mobile device as a controller for an online game of competitive snake! The game itself is rendered using HTML5 canvas and javascript, while controls are communicated through HTML5 websockets. Additionally, Google fast-buttons are used to reduce the latency of controls on mobile devices.

Air-Rise

Facebook 2013 Midwest Regional Hackathon Entry! Using similar technology to Toss, Air-rise allows users to take images through a mobile app and send it instantly to a computer's clipboard via a computer client running in the background. Computer client is cross-platform, and comes complete with fancy notifications when an image is recieved! A modified version of Base64 encoding is also used to ensure a quick transmit time.

Bounce

A small billiards-style iOS game built on Cocos2D that I work on in my free time. Also still in the works, so I'm gonna stay a little hush.

This Website!

My baby, and first real foray into anything web-related. So far it consists of some rather ugly looking CSS and HTML (click inspect element, I dare you) that somehow works. I've also taken the time to add some sparkly jQuery elements and implement some light reactive programming techniques for the mobile version.
