Projects

Whether for work, school, or play, I love creating! Here are a few of my favorite projects from recent memory. You can check out an even better profile of my work and the people I love working with at my GitHub page!

VisDir

A weekend project of mine to learn and explore the potential of D3.js. VisDir creates an interactive visualization of an uploaded directory. I'll continue contributing small things to it here and there, although I'm already pretty happy with its state as a fun tool. You can play around with it yourself here!

Stache

A project for my UI/UX class, Stache is a chat client that attempts to solve the problem of important messages being lost in a large backlog. Every sent message is parsed for information and, if a match is found, is automatically placed into a predefined category for later use. Messages can also be saved into custom categories manually. As part of a team of 5, I helped build Stache in the span of a couple weeks following extensive research of its application and usability. I was able to exercise my MVC knowledge a bit by heading up the front-end development, which was done in Backbone.js.

OCRa

Built during MHacks, Spring 2014, I worked in a team of 3 to build OCRa, a mobile app that uses Google Tesseract to find digital versions of hard media, such as a resume or newspaper article. I headed up the design and mobile development, while my teammates built a backend to search for matching articles.

Classkick

Summer of my sophomore year, I was a software engineering intern for the 1871 startup, Classkick. Classkick is a mobile education platform designed to give students instant and effective feedback, and teachers a more global scope of their classroom. I worked on a team of 3 in a fast-paced development environment, in which we implemented core features and infrastructure to carry the app its launch in Fall 2014. Check them out at getclasskick.com, or download it for iPad here!

Nerf-Admin

Built for BattleHack Chicago 2014, Nerf-Admin attempts to streamline the process of hosting large scale nerf-battles for clubs and organizations. Features included automated game start/stop texts via Twilio, and nerf-gun rental through BrainTree. I handled the design and support for special game modes like Assassins. We ended up winning the Twilio prize for best use of the Twilio API!

Toss

Worked in a team of 3 in a hackathon to develop mobile and desktop clients for Toss. Toss allows users to draw and send images from a mobile device to a computer's clipboard instantly! My work encompassed the mobile development aspect as well as designing a network protocol between desktop and mobile clients. We ended up taking first place for the IEEE CapitalOne 2013 Hackathon! I continue to research better tech for Toss in my free time, and intend to have this published in the near future.

Armagetra-Snake

Powered by HTML5 and Jetty, Armagetra-Snake uses your mobile device as a controller for an online game of competitive snake! The game itself is rendered using HTML5 canvas and javascript, while controls are communicated through HTML5 websockets. Additionally, Google fast-buttons are used to reduce the latency of controls on mobile devices. This project was more an exploration into websockets and network communication than anything else, but was super fun and turned out really nicely!

Air-Rise

Facebook 2013 Midwest Regional Hackathon Entry! Using similar technology to Toss, Air-rise allows users to take images through a mobile app and send it instantly to a computer's clipboard via a desktop client running in the background. The desktop client is cross-platform, and comes complete with fancy notifications when an image is recieved! We also used our own modified version of Base 64 encoding to reduce network communication time.

Bounce

A small billiards-style iOS game built on top of Cocos2D during my freshman summer. I continue to work on it occasionally, although am unlikely to publish it. In addition to teaching myself iOS development during this project, I also learned how to design custom assets and built a light, albeit crude, physics engine to handle the billard collisions.
